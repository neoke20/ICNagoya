I.C.Nagoya introduction

35 years experience

I.C.Nagoya has been established and teaching Japanese for more than 35 years. We leverage that experience and continuously improve our teaching methods, to make sure you get the best out of your time studying with us.

Location

I.C.Nagoya is located right next to the Nagoya station. Nagoya station is one of the largest train station in the world. This means you can easily travel all over Japan, using Nagoya as your base. And not matter where your accommodation is, it will be easy to commute to the school.

Activites

I.C.Nagoya regularly organizes off-campus learning and exciting events. Studying is very important, but what best, to learn a language, than to also immerse yourself in the local culture, and enjoy all it has to offer!

EJU

I.C.Nagoya will prepare you for the EJU (Examination for Japanese University), and admission exam for international students. Because you have goals for your future, and we know that this could be a life-changing experience.

Job hunt

I.C.Nagoya is one of the few insitutions selected by the Japanese government to allow its students to stay in Japan to job hunt for up to 1 year after their graduation. Because choosing the right job should not be done in a hurry.

Support

Thanks to our 35-year-experience, we are able to provide academic as well as life support to the students that join us. Need help with settling in Japan? Want to organize a trip and need advices? We got you covered!

Our extra services

Free counseling with teachers and staff members

Careful attention, full support and professional advice is provided through regular personal interviews with teachers and staff members.

Free education seminars

In the first semester, entrance examiners of universities, graduate schools and vocational schools are invited to hold information sessions at the school.

Academic and employment counseling support available

Guidance and full support is provided so students reach their goals developing their abilities.

Many information sessions are held to guide students not only on the Japanese language study, life in Japan and future career, but also to support them on part-time job and employment. Job interview practice classes are also available.

I.C.NAGOYA Scholarship

This is a learning incentive for students with excellent attendance, grades, and learning attitudes. Selected students receive a total of 30,000 yen for a three-month term. Up to 20 students are selected every year.

Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO)

Outstanding students who continue higher education in Japan may be introduced to the Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO) to get a "scholarship" every month while studying at the universities or vocational schools in Japan.

School staff go to Chubu International Airport (NGO) to pick-up new students. From the airport to the school, it takes about 28 minutes and it is a free service. Students are asked, if possible, to choose a flight that arrives on weekdays. Students have to pay only their own one-way fee to Nagoya station that costs 1,250 yen.
